Course Description

This course is an introduction to college physics, using an algebra-based approach. The course covers a range of topics, concepts, and theories in general physics including kinematics and dynamics in 1D and 2D motion, forces and Newton's laws of motion, work and energy, impulse and momentum, rotational kinematics and dynamics, simple and harmonic motion, fluid dynamics, and temperature and heat. The course also introduces the student to applied physics using real-world engineering problems and laboratory exercises.

Course Objectives

· Express measurements and calculations with accurate values and units.

· Solve kinematic problems using both quantitative and graphical methods.

· Apply Newton's laws of motion to solving problems involving equilibrium, net force and uniform circular motion.

· Use the work-energy theorem and conservation of energy

· Solve collision problems involving mass, velocity, momentum, and the conservation of linear momentum.

· Analyze rotational motion using torque, moment of inertia and angular acceleration.

· Use Archimedes Principle and Bernoulli's Equation to solve problems involving fluids and fluid dynamics.

· Analyze the motion of an object in simple harmonic motion.

· Calculate the amount of heat required to change the temperature or phase of a material.

· Analyze the results of lab experiments on a variety of physics concepts.

Credit Hours: 4

Prerequisites: MA114 or MA141
