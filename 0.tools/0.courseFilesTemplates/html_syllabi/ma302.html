Course Description

This course provides an introduction to calculus. Topics include limits, derivatives, concavity, applications of the derivative, integration, applications of integrations, the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus, and integrating using parts and substitutions.

Course Objectives

·       Explain how the principles of calculus can be applied in everyday life

·       Evaluate and interpret limits graphically, numerically, and analytically

·       Explain the definition of the derivative and relate it to average rate of change and slope

·       Apply differentiation rules and explain the purpose of differentiation

·       Demonstrate the ability to analyze the graph of a function by applying the first and second derivatives

·       Apply the derivate to solve applications involving optimization and related rates

·       Explain and apply the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus

·       Apply the process antidifferention and explain its purpose

·       Apply the process of integration and explain its purpose

·       Apply relevant theorems and formulae to solve problems

Credit Hours: 4

Prerequisites: MA141
