Course Description

This intermediate mathematics course presents methods in making analytical decisions using statistics. This course focuses on the characteristics of numerical and categorical data, methods of presentation, and descriptive statistics. Basic probability theory and probability distributions will be introduced in this course. Correlation and covariance are presented in the context of business analysis. The course also introduces students to basic methods of sampling and of making inferences using one or two independent samples.

 

Course Objectives

 

·        Describe the role of statistics in business decision-making.

·        Identify the major sources and types of data.

·        Appropriately organize numerical data using tables, charts, and graphs.

·        Use descriptive statistics in the context of exploratory data analysis.

·        Apply basic probability concept and describe the role of probability in Statistics.

·        Apply basic discrete probability distributions including Binomial Probability distribution and Poisson probability distribution.

·        Describe the importance of the central limit theorem.

·        Calculate confidence intervals and determine sample sizes.

·        Define and demonstrate hypothesis testing using one-sample tests.
