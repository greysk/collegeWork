Course Description

This course examines the legal environment pertinent to security professionals. Topics include the role of government, relevant civil and criminal law, constitutional rights and privacy issues, intellectual property, and compliance. In addition, current trends in cybersecurity are explored.

Course Objectives

         Demonstrate a working knowledge of federal and state law relevant to information security

         Delineate the relationship between enterprise information security and national security

         Analyze the impact of privacy laws and intellectual property laws on enterprise policies and compliance requirements

         Evaluate conflicts between security interests of individuals, private enterprise, and government bodies

         Address the impact of cyberspace on the concept of jurisdiction

         Apply knowledge gained throughout program to address current trends in cybersecurity

Credit Hours: 3

Prerequisites: None
